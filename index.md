__Hi!__ I'm Michael Goulet. I am currently a student studying Computer Science at Caltech in Pasadena, CA. This is my personal site that I hope to use to keep track of my programming endeavors and record what I'm up to.

I enjoy programming in C++, Rust, and Scala. Topic-wise, I particularly like studying compilers, operating systems, and computer graphics, though I've done quite a lot of work on database implementation in Scala as well. A few of my favorite projects at Caltech include _Arrow_, as Scala database query optimizer, and _Chesire_, which was a Rust-like programming language compiler written in Rust.

I'm currently at Facebook as a Production Engineer Intern working on Infrastructure under the Posix Storage team. We work on a slew of things, with a focus on enabling the open source storage solution GlusterFS at Facebook. My projects currently are centered around QoS on Gluster, though I'm working close with my team on taking a stab at new ideas that are in the pipeline. I hope to upstream my work so the GlusterFS community can benefit, so keep an eye out for my commits!

Last summer (2016), I worked at Nasa's Jet Propulsion Laboratory doing software development through a Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship in the Marine Autonomy lab. I worked on improving the stability of the development lifecycle for the various customers using our team's API, and fixed a few crucial bugs and improved the reliability of an important Message Passing framework used on our robots. During the Summer 2015, I worked at Hewlett-Packard doing Linux Software Engineering working on thin client devices and operating system virtualization, culminating with a few exciting demos and some of the projects I worked on inspiring and being integrated into current products marketed by HP today.
