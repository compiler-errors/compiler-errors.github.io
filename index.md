## Who am I?

I'm Michael Goulet. I was born and grew up in Houston, TX. Some things that I love are computers, functional programming, compilers, Magical Realism fiction, contemporary art, and Italian greyhounds.

I currently work at Facebook as a Production Engineer on a team called Storage Management. I spend my day yelling at hard drives and making sure a vast fleet of HDD and SSD-based storage devices are healthy and happy.

In my free time, I've been hacking on a toy programming language called Cheshire, and I'm working on the second implementation of the compiler called Adelaide. [Check it out](https://github.com/compiler-errors/adelaide).

## Education and Interests

I studied Computer Science and English at Caltech in Pasadena, California. I was a member of Ruddock and Ricketts houses. At Caltech, I really enjoyed taking and TAing the Databases track of classes, and I was one of the first TAs for a class focused on the implementation of interpreters. I wrote my [English thesis](https://thesis.library.caltech.edu/11740/) on homosexuality in the '50s and '60s.

If I were to go back to school, I'd interested in the intersection of programming languages and their impact on the ergonomics of developing systems that scale various of programming language paradigms, for example databases which could uilize both highly functional programming (e.g. in an optimizer) and C/Go-like imperative programming (e.g. in its near-filesystem APIs). Designing a system or common runtime to facilitate interoperability between a set of programming languages that span several programming paradigms would be really neat.
